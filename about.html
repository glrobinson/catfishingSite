About This Project

This website was developed as part of Practicum in HUA: Wasteworks. The goal of the project was to showcase the harm that trash has on our environment, specifically the ocean, by using recyclable materials to create a visual representation of a fish tank. With this website we hope to teach you how to recycle, so that less waste ends up in the ocean.

Purpose

Help people recycle correctly (and avoid wishcycling) with short, clear rules.

How you learn

Quick feedback loops: you make a choice, get the reason, and remember it next time.

What we hope for you to get out of this website

Understanding how to recycle correctly and fewer wrong items contaminating entire loads.

Core rules

1) Empty + quick rinse

Food residue is one of the biggest causes of contamination.

2) Avoid film plastics curbside

Bags and wraps often jam sorting machines unless there’s a special drop-off.

3) When in doubt, keep it out

Better to trash one questionable item than contaminate the entire bin.

4) Local rules matter

Accepted plastics and glass rules vary — verify using the Resources page.

FAQ

Is this accurate everywhere?
Programs vary by city. This site gives solid defaults, and Resources provides trusted places to verify.
What is wishcycling?
Tossing items in recycling “just in case.” It often increases contamination and sorting costs.
